Placement Training and Enrichment Activities – July to September 2025

The Department of Networking and Communications, SRM Institute of Science and Technology, organized a comprehensive series of Placement Training and Career Enrichment Programs from July to September 2025 to enhance the employability skills and industry readiness of students. These initiatives aimed to bridge the gap between academic learning and professional requirements through a blend of mock tests, technical sessions, and expert interactions.

The program commenced with the Placement Mock Test Series 05 – General Aptitude Test 01 on 26th July 2025, which enabled 324 students from II, III, and IV years to assess their quantitative, logical, and analytical abilities — crucial for placement exams. This was followed by a Virtual Talk on “Gateway to Industry” on 30th July 2025, delivered by Mr. A. Anantharaman, Scientific Officer, National Institute of Science and Research, Bhubaneswar, attended by 95 participants, providing valuable insights into industry expectations and career pathways. The Soft Skills Test 01 conducted on 6th August 2025 for 780 students emphasized communication and interpersonal competencies essential for interviews and group discussions.

Subsequently, the Reasoning Test 01 held on 9th August 2025 with 923 participants focused on strengthening analytical and logical reasoning skills. A Virtual Talk on Higher Studies Opportunities on 13th August 2025 engaged 45 students, guiding them toward global higher education and research options. Another session on Gateway to Industry on 20th August 2025 by Dr. K. Venkataraman, Technical Architect, Hexaware, attended by 40 students, highlighted current industry trends and professional growth strategies. The Hackerrank Test 01 conducted on 23rd August 2025 engaged 681 students in coding-based assessments to test programming and problem-solving abilities.

Continuing the series, the General Aptitude Test 02 on 30th August 2025 saw 835 participants refining their time management and numerical skills. On 3rd September 2025, another Virtual Talk on Higher Studies Opportunities for 52 students focused on academic progression through advanced technical education. The Technical Test 02 held on 6th September 2025 for 533 students assessed their domain-specific knowledge essential for placement drives. The Reasoning Test 02 on 13th September 2025, with 619 participants, further strengthened logical reasoning skills relevant for competitive exams and interviews.

Towards the end of the program, a Virtual Talk on Higher Studies Pathways on 17th September 2025 guided 58 students on postgraduate programs and scholarships abroad. The Hackerrank Test 02 conducted on 20th September 2025 for 601 students provided hands-on problem-solving experience in coding. Finally, the series concluded with the General Aptitude Test 03 on 27th September 2025, where 506 students evaluated their overall readiness for campus recruitment processes. Overall, the structured blend of mock assessments, technical evaluations, and interactive expert sessions significantly contributed to improving students’ placement preparedness, confidence, and professional orientation.

PG Students Placement Training Report

Between August and September 2025, the department recorded consistent student engagement across the scheduled training activities. The session-wise participation analysis showed that final-year students maintained an impressive average attendance of 65.6% per session across eight sessions, while first-year students achieved an average of 50.2% attendance over six sessions, having joined the program from 22nd August 2025 onward.

During this period, a total of eight training sessions were conducted between 8th August and 26th September 2025, encompassing a comprehensive mix of learning formats. The coverage by session type indicated that Coding Sessions and Technical Training were conducted consistently across all eight sessions, achieving 100% coverage each. In addition, Aptitude and Quantitative Training was delivered in two sessions (25%), specifically on 8th and 15th August 2025.

Across all sessions, the recorded learning outcomes reflected positive skill enhancement, with participants demonstrating improved quantitative abilities, enhanced coding proficiency, and greater technical competence, underscoring the effectiveness of the training initiatives.
