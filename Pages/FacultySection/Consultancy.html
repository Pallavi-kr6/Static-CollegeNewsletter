About the Consultancy Work

The consultancy focuses on developing an AI-based solution for the early detection of diabetic retinopathy, a vision-threatening complication of diabetes that affects millions globally. The project harnesses the power of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to automatically detect abnormalities in retinal images captured through fundus cameras. By training the CNN model on large datasets, the system learns to distinguish between different stages of retinopathy - from mild non-proliferative changes to severe proliferative conditions - with remarkable accuracy. This innovative collaboration bridges healthcare and artificial intelligence, bringing together medical experts and machine learning specialists. The developed system is designed to assist ophthalmologists by offering a second opinion through image-based diagnostic analytics, thereby improving both speed and precision in the screening process. The consultancy also aims to reduce manual dependency and make screening more accessible and affordable, especially in rural and semi-urban healthcare setups. In addition to its technical depth, this initiative contributes to social impact, ensuring early diagnosis for diabetic patients and preventing avoidable vision loss. The collaboration exemplifies how interdisciplinary synergy — between computing and healthcare — can yield solutions that not only advance technology but also serve humanity.
The project deliverables include:
• Development of an optimized CNN model with high detection accuracy.
• Integration of a real-time image processing pipeline.
• Prototype deployment for testing in clinical environments.
• Comprehensive documentation and training support for the collaborating agency.
Impact and Significance:
This consultancy embodies the department’s commitment to innovation-driven social transformation. It reflects how emerging AI technologies can be tailored to address pressing healthcare challenges. Beyond technical contributions, it also sets a model for effective Academic–industry collaboration, enhancing SRM’s reputation as a hub of applied research excellence. Through such projects, faculty and students are encouraged to align their research with global sustainability and health objectives, fostering a culture of purposeful innovation.
